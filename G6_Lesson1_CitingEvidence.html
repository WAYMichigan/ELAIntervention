What Does It Mean to Cite Evidence?
When you analyze a text, you can't just say what you think — you have to prove it with words from the text itself. That's called citing evidence.
📌 The Big Idea

There are two kinds of evidence you can cite from a text:

Explicit evidence — the text says it directly. You can point right to the words.

Implicit evidence — the text implies it. You have to read between the lines and explain the connection.

✦ Model — Explicit Evidence

Text says: "Kezia crossed her arms and refused to answer any of the judge's questions."

Analysis: According to the text, Kezia "refused to answer any of the judge's questions." This directly shows that she is being defiant and uncooperative.

✦ Model — Implicit Evidence

Text says: "Kezia stared at the floor during the entire hearing, her hands balled into fists."

Analysis: The text describes Kezia's fists and downward gaze. This implies she is feeling either angry or afraid — the text never states it directly, but the physical details suggest a strong emotional reaction.

✦ Sentence Frame — Use This Every Time

According to the text, "[exact quote]." This [explicitly shows / implies / suggests] that [your analysis] because [your reasoning].

📄 Practice Text — "The Long Way Back"

After the accident, no one expected Dara to compete again. She had been the fastest sprinter in the district — and now, at sixteen, she was learning to walk without a limp. Her physical therapist told her it was possible to run again, but it would take months of painful work with no guarantee.

Dara arrived at every session before her therapist did. She kept a notebook where she recorded her daily distances — 12 steps on Monday, 15 on Tuesday, 19 on Wednesday. She did not celebrate small numbers. She just wrote them down and came back the next day.

"I don't think about the race," she told her mother one evening. "I think about tomorrow's number." Her mother wiped her eyes and said nothing.

By spring, Dara was running. Not fast — not yet. But she was running.

Question 1 of 3
Which sentence from the text explicitly shows that Dara is determined to recover?
Question 2 of 3
The text says Dara "did not celebrate small numbers." What does this implicitly suggest about her?
Question 3 of 3

Task 1
Find one piece of explicit evidence from the text that shows Dara's resilience. Use the sentence frame to write your response.
💡 Model: According to the text, Dara "arrived at every session before her therapist did." This explicitly shows that she is committed to her recovery because she is taking initiative and going beyond what is required of her.
Task 2
Find one piece of implicit evidence from the text. Quote it, label it as implicit, and explain what it suggests and why.
💡 Model: According to the text, Dara's mother "wiped her eyes and said nothing." This implies that her mother is deeply moved — possibly a combination of pride and sadness — because the text never states her feelings directly, but a parent wiping their eyes in silence is a powerful signal of strong emotion.
