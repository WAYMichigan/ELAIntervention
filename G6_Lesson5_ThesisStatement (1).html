What Is a Thesis Statement?
A thesis statement is one sentence that tells your reader your position AND previews your main reasons. It's the foundation of your entire argument.
📌 The Formula

Opinion + "because" + Reason 1, Reason 2, and Reason 3

A strong thesis does three things: it takes a clear position, it gives specific reasons (not vague ones), and it can be argued against — if no one could disagree with it, it's not a real argument.

✗ Weak Thesis (too vague)

"Resilience is important and everyone should have it."

Why weak: No specific reasons. No one could argue against this. It's a statement of fact, not an argument.

✓ Strong Thesis

"Resilience is a skill that anyone can develop because it grows through repeated challenges, it can be taught through deliberate practice, and real-world examples prove it is not a fixed trait."

Why strong: Takes a position someone could disagree with, gives three specific reasons, previews the argument.

✦ Thesis Formula

[Topic] is [your position] because [Reason 1], [Reason 2], and [Reason 3].

You Do — Independent Practice
Write Your Own Thesis
Choose one of the topics below and write a thesis statement using the formula. Then write a second thesis for a different topic.
Choose a topic (or use your own):

• Students should be allowed to use their phones in class

• Community service should be required for graduation

• Schools should start later in the morning

• Athletes should be held to higher academic standards

✦ Thesis Formula

[Topic] is/should be [your position] because [Reason 1], [Reason 2], and [Reason 3].

Task 1 — Write Your First Thesis
Choose one topic and write a strong thesis statement using the formula above.
💡 Model (schools starting later): School start times should be pushed to 8:30 AM or later because adolescent sleep science shows that teenagers are biologically wired to stay up and wake up later, sleep deprivation measurably reduces academic performance, and schools that have already made the shift report higher grades and lower absenteeism.
Task 2 — Check Your Own Thesis
Answer these three questions about the thesis you just wrote. Type your answers below.
1. Does it take a clear position? 2. Does it give three specific reasons? 3. Could someone disagree with it?
