I Do — Watch and Learn
What Makes a Summary Objective?
A summary reports what a text says. An objective summary does that without adding your own opinions, reactions, or feelings. Think of yourself as a reporter, not a reviewer.
📌 The Big Idea

An objective summary has three things:

1. The central idea — what the text is mainly about

2. The key supporting details — the most important evidence or examples

3. No personal opinion — no "I think," "I feel," "I believe," or "this is interesting"

✗ Not Objective (Opinion Included)

"This article was really interesting because it talks about a community garden that I think everyone should support. The people working on it are very inspiring and it's amazing what they accomplished."

✓ Objective Summary

"This article describes the creation of a community garden in Detroit's Eastside neighborhood. Residents converted an abandoned lot into a working garden that now provides produce for over 40 families. The article explains the challenges the group faced — including funding and soil contamination — and how they resolved each one over two growing seasons."

✦ Sentence Frame — Objective Summary

This [article / text / passage] explains [topic]. The author describes [key detail 1] and [key detail 2]. By the end, the reader understands that [main takeaway].

📄 Practice Text — "Seeds in the Concrete"

In 2018, a group of high school students in Flint, Michigan launched a project called GrowFlint — a student-run urban farming initiative aimed at addressing food insecurity in their neighborhood. Working with a local nonprofit, the students transformed three vacant lots into productive growing spaces.

In the first year, GrowFlint produced over 800 pounds of vegetables, which were distributed free of charge at a weekly market. The students managed every aspect of the operation: planting schedules, soil testing, volunteer coordination, and community outreach. Two of the original founders were offered college scholarships specifically for their leadership in the project.

Research from the University of Michigan found that neighborhoods with community growing projects show measurable improvements in social cohesion and reduced food insecurity within three years of launch. GrowFlint has since expanded to five locations across the city.

📄 New Practice Text — "The Last Library"

When budget cuts threatened to close the Riverside Branch Library in 2021, a coalition of local residents organized a campaign to save it. Over eight weeks, they collected more than 3,000 signatures, held community reading events, and presented cost-saving proposals to the city council.

The council ultimately voted to keep the library open and approved a reduced but sustainable budget. Library usage increased by 22% in the following year, driven largely by new after-school programs the community had proposed as part of their campaign. The organizers credited their success to clear documentation of the library's impact on underserved families in the district.
