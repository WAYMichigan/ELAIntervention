I Do — Watch and Learn
Why Does Word Choice Matter?
Authors choose every word on purpose. The specific word an author picks — instead of a simpler or more neutral word — shapes how you feel while reading and what you understand about characters, settings, and ideas.
📌 Two Key Terms

Denotation — the dictionary definition of a word. What it literally means.

Connotation — the emotional feeling or association a word carries beyond its literal meaning.

Example: "cheap" and "economical" both mean low cost. But "cheap" feels negative (low quality), while "economical" feels positive (smart spending). Same denotation. Very different connotation.

✦ Model Analysis

Sentence: "The old house crouched at the end of the lane."

Word to analyze: "crouched"

Denotation: bent low, hunched down

Connotation: threatening, lurking, animal-like — as if the house is waiting to pounce

Effect on tone: Creates a sinister, unsettling atmosphere before we've even entered the house

✦ Sentence Frame — Word Choice Analysis

The word "[word]" literally means [denotation]. In this context, it suggests [connotation / emotional association]. This creates a [tone word: dark / hopeful / threatening / lonely / etc.] tone because [reasoning].

We Do — Guided Practice
Read, Find, Analyze
Read the paragraph below. The underlined words are your focus. Answer the questions about each one.
📄 Practice Text — "First Chair"

The concert hall swallowed Marcus whole the moment he stepped through its doors. Three thousand seats. A chandelier that looked like it had been stolen from a palace. And at the center of the stage — alone under a single light — his violin waited for him like an accusation.

He had practiced this piece for eleven months. He knew every note, every breath, every shift of the bow. And yet his hands were traitors, shaking slightly as he tuned the strings backstage. His teacher placed a hand on his shoulder and said something. Marcus did not hear the words. He was already on the stage in his mind, already falling.

Question 1 of 3
The text says the concert hall "swallowed" Marcus. What does this word choice suggest?
Question 2 of 3
Marcus calls his hands "traitors." What tone does this word choice create?
Question 3 of 3
Which response best analyzes the word "accusation" in the phrase "his violin waited for him like an accusation"?
You Do — Independent Practice
Analyze Two Word Choices
Read the new paragraph below. Choose two bolded words and complete the sentence frame for each.
📄 New Practice Text — "The Interview"

Priya had rehearsed her answers forty-seven times. She had a number for it — forty-seven — because she had been counting every session in her notebook since March. Now, sitting in the waiting room of the law firm, surrounded by people in suits that cost more than her mother's monthly rent, she felt her confidence dissolve like sugar in rain.

The receptionist called her name. Priya stood. She was eighteen years old, the daughter of immigrants, wearing a blazer she had borrowed from her debate coach, and she was about to walk into a room and convince three partners that she deserved a spot in their summer program. She straightened her spine and walked through the door.

✦ Sentence Frame

The word "[word]" literally means [denotation]. In this context, it suggests [connotation]. This creates a [tone word] tone because [reasoning].

Task 1 — First Word Choice
Choose one of the bolded words from the text. Use the sentence frame to write your word choice analysis.
💡 Model (using "dissolve"): The word "dissolve" literally means to break down or disappear into a liquid. In this context, it suggests that Priya's confidence is fragile and temporary — it disappears the moment it meets pressure, just as sugar disappears in rain. This creates a vulnerable, anxious tone because it shows that even all of Priya's preparation cannot protect her from self-doubt in the moment.
Task 2 — Second Word Choice
Choose a second bolded word and complete the frame again.
💡 Model (using "borrowed"): The word "borrowed" literally means taken temporarily from someone else. Here it suggests that Priya doesn't fully belong in this world — she is borrowing not just a blazer but an identity, a costume that isn't really hers. This creates a tone of quiet tension between who Priya is and the world she is trying to enter.
